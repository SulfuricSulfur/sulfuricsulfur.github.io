Aisles of Chaos

Aisle of Chaos game made for the RIT 2015 fall Hack-A-Thon. Won for best multiplayer game. Group size was 4. All the artwork for the game was done by me. The point of the game is to get more points than the other player by bringing as many products to the checkout counter as you can. Each product also acts as a weapon, but using it means it disappears from your inventory. Hitting the other player with a product makes them stunned for a few seconds and all items in their inventory are dropped. Each product has a different function from watermelon bombs(melon-bomber) to bear trap oranges (mandarin mantrap).
