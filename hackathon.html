Humdinger

Humdinger is an AR project made during the 2018 American Greeting Intern Hack-a-Thon. Group size was 3. Won first place. Was responsible for programming and setting up the AR.
Humdinger is a line of AR greeting cards that can work with already existing greeting cards that are part of the American Greeting card and patches line. AR cards and patches were designed that if two different AR cards were put within a certain proximity of each other they would interact. 3 Different cards were made.

  • Star and moon patches: By themselves, the star and the moon will display a 3D model. If both are within proximity of each other then mini-stars will spawn out of the big star and follow the moon until the moon is no longer on the AR camera.
  • Unicorn and rainbow: By themselves, the unicorn and rainbow will have a particle affect around each image. When both are put in proximity of each other then it will trigger a larger explosion of particles.
  • Portal to winterland: This requires the AR picture of the key and a door. The door will display a closed brown door while the key will display a 3D key. Once the key gets close enough to the door, the door will do away revealing a portal to a winter wonderland. The user can peak inside the portal on flat surfaces.

Aisles of Chaos

Aisle of Chaos game made for the RIT 2015 fall Hack-A-Thon. Won for best multiplayer game. Group size was 4. The artwork for the game was done by me. The objective of the game is to get more points than the other player by bringing as many products to the checkout counter as you can. Each product also acts as a weapon, but using it means it disappears from your inventory. Hitting the other player with a product makes them stunned for a few seconds and all items in their inventory are dropped. Each product has a different function from watermelon bombs(melon-bomber) to bear trap oranges (mandarin mantrap).
